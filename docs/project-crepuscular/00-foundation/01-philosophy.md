# Philosophy

**Status:** Approved  
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose

Vesper exists to make the real sky understandable, observable, and worth sharing.

It is not built to replace observation with a screen. It prepares people to step away from the screen with confidence.

## The Product Relationship

Vesper should feel useful without becoming demanding.

The product earns trust by being accurate, quiet, and honest. It does not manufacture urgency. It does not inflate ordinary events. It does not punish users for leaving.

The healthiest Vesper session may be brief:

1. Open.
2. Understand tonight.
3. Know where and when to look.
4. Close.
5. Observe.

## The Observatory Principle

Vesper should feel like an observatory rather than a generic dashboard.

That means:

- information is precise
- hierarchy replaces decoration
- darkness creates focus
- light communicates meaning
- motion explains change
- completion is visible
- empty space is intentional

The interface should feel like an instrument, but never like a control panel designed to impress.

## Shared Discovery

The final outcome is not private knowledge.

The user should be able to retell what they learned in one sentence and point at the sky with confidence.

Discovery becomes valuable when it is shared.

## Trust

Trust is built through restraint:

- silence on cloudy nights
- clear source attribution
- no guessed certainty
- no notification flooding
- no manipulative paywall behavior
- no false urgency
- no exaggerated language

## Closing Principle

> Every pixel should help someone spend more time looking up than looking down.

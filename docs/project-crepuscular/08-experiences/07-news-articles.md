# News and Articles

**Status:** Draft for review  
**Version:** 0.1

## Purpose

News carries the user's mind beyond the local sky without becoming an endless engagement feed.

## Core Principle

> Inform, attribute, finish.

## Feed

The feed should prioritize:

- significant discoveries
- missions
- launches
- events connected to what users can observe
- clear primary-source attribution

It should not optimize for infinite scrolling.

## Feed Structure

Recommended:

1. one featured story
2. recent relevant stories
3. mission or event bridge
4. clear end or loading boundary

Avoid an uninterrupted stream of equal cards.

## Article Card

Show:

- image
- category
- title
- short summary
- source
- publication date

Do not use sensational badges or urgency unless the information is genuinely time-sensitive.

## Article Structure

Recommended Q&A pattern:

1. What happened?
2. Why does it matter?
3. Can I see it?
4. What happens next?
5. Source

The answer should be retellable in one sentence.

## Voice

- smart friend, not press release
- scientific body first
- no fabricated certainty
- at most one poetic closing line
- no clickbait
- no unexplained jargon

## Sources

Every article shows:

- source organization
- source line or link
- publication context
- image credit where required

Vesper never implies ownership of external telescopes or discoveries.

## Translation

The meaning and certainty must remain equivalent across:

- pt-PT
- pt-BR
- English
- Spanish

Do not translate technical names inconsistently.

## Notifications

News alerts are off by default.

When enabled:

- send only meaningful stories
- commit article before push
- deep-link to the available article
- respect quiet hours
- do not use breaking-news language casually

## Reading Experience

- solid reading surface
- constrained line width
- no moving star field
- clear source
- comfortable type
- progress is optional
- related content appears after the article, not between paragraphs

## Offline

Saved or cached articles may remain readable offline.

The feed clearly distinguishes unavailable remote content from local failure.

## Accessibility

- images have meaningful descriptions
- headings form a logical outline
- source is reachable
- body supports Dynamic Type
- no essential information is embedded only in imagery

## Anti-Patterns

Do not:

- build an endless feed
- hide sources
- use clickbait
- interrupt articles with paywalls or review prompts
- push routine news by default
- use a cinematic background behind long reading

# The Soul of Vesper

**Status:** Approved  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Canonical since:** 2026-07-15

Seven questions, answered once. Every screen, notification, store listing, and product decision answers to this section.

## North Star

> Vesper does not try to keep people inside the app. It brings them back to the sky.

The application is not the destination. The sky is.

Success is not measured only by minutes spent in Vesper. It is measured by whether someone closes it ready to look up, understand what they see, and share that knowledge with the person beside them.

## 1. Why Vesper Is Special

Vesper turns the night sky into a daily habit of curiosity.

First-time stargazers gain knowledge and trust. Serious observers gain precision. Everyone gains something worth pointing at.

It earns daily use honestly because the sky changes every day. It respects two rare things equally:

- the night
- the user's attention

Vesper does not merely tell people what is above them. It teaches them to notice it. Over time, strangers become observers, observers become guides, and discovery becomes something naturally shared.

## 2. The First Open

The first open should create sparkle: wonder, excitement, and the feeling that the universe is suddenly within reach.

Day one is cinematic. The introduction is allowed to amaze.

Day thirty is calm anticipation:

> Jupiter at 22:40, and the sky will be clear.

**The intro dazzles; the ritual soothes.**

## 3. The 22:00 Feeling

Awe with no noise.

Stars are being born and dying overhead. Everyday problems return to their true scale. A distant dot becomes:

> That is Jupiter.

The emotional result is peace, orientation, and an exhale.

## 4. The Place

Vesper is a rooftop at dusk, kept by a lighthouse, with a spacecraft's horizon.

- **The rooftop** is where the user stands: democratic, one flight above ordinary life, with no special gear required.
- **The lighthouse** is the alert engine: someone kept watch so the user did not have to.
- **The spacecraft** is the feed: the mind carried into the vast unknown.

Vesper is never a planetarium. It points at the real sky.

## 5. The Person

Vesper is an old sailor from the age of celestial navigation fused with an astronaut adrift in the dark: the past and future of wayfinding in one voice.

Vesper:

- speaks little
- points often
- explains through realistic drawings, glyphs, directions, and short text
- is scientific and direct
- becomes slightly poetic only at the end
- is quiet and patient
- uses humor rarely and dryly
- is honest about clouds

## 6. Vesper Never

Vesper never:

- charges people for what is above their heads
- shouts twice
- guesses
- begs for reviews, upgrades, or one more minute
- spends attention without paying it back in sky
- pretends poor observing conditions are good

> Free is complete, forever, for everyone — with or without knowledge.

> Measured, not guessed.

## 7. On Closing

The user should feel ready.

Ready to look up. Ready to explain the sky to whoever is standing beside them. Quietly hungry to return.

Being closed is Vesper succeeding.

Every user who leaves ready to teach is part of the growth loop.

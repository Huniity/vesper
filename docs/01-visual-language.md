# Voice

**Status:** Approved  
**Version:** 1.0

## Voice Summary

Vesper speaks like a quiet celestial navigator.

It is scientific and direct first. It may become slightly poetic at the end, but never begins with poetry when the user needs information.

## Core Rule

> Point, do not lecture.

Lead with a glyph, object, direction, time, measurement, or diagram. Text is short and secondary.

## Structure

1. State the scientific fact.
2. Explain what the user should do.
3. Optionally close with one poetic line.

## Registers

### Onboarding and Eclipse Moments

May sparkle.

These moments can carry more emotion, scale, and cinematic language because they introduce the universe or mark a rare event.

### Daily Surfaces

Stay quiet.

Daily copy should be fast to scan, easy to repeat, and honest about conditions.

### Notifications and Alerts

Must be concise and actionable.

Alerts never contain jokes, decorative poetry, or exaggerated urgency.

## Humor

Humor is:

- rare
- dry
- one line at most
- never used in safety information
- never used in urgent alerts
- never used to minimize poor conditions

## Conditions

Clouds win sometimes. Say it plainly.

Example:

> Hoje o céu não colabora.

Do not disguise poor conditions with optimistic language.

## Retellability

Write so the user can explain the result to another person in one sentence.

Sharing is the endgame.

## Voice by Context

- **Philosophy:** expressive
- **Marketing:** inspiring but credible
- **Onboarding:** warm and cinematic
- **Interface:** precise and compact
- **Data:** literal and unambiguous
- **Notifications:** actionable
- **Safety:** direct and absolute
- **Errors:** calm and useful

## Do

- use exact times and directions
- state uncertainty when it exists
- make the next action obvious
- prefer ordinary language over specialist jargon
- explain technical terms only when needed

## Do Not

- open functional copy with poetry
- exaggerate ordinary events
- manufacture urgency
- claim certainty without evidence
- ask repeatedly for engagement
- use jokes in alerts
- hide poor conditions
